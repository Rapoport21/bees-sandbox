# Bees — Design Context

> Provided once and re-used by all design-skill invocations
> (`impeccable`, `polish`, `critique`, `delight`, etc.). Don't infer
> design intent from code — read this.

## Design Context

### Users

**Primary**: 30–45-year-old urban professionals who garden, care about
provenance, and have disposable income for a $15–50/month subscription.
They are curious by nature, value craft and origin stories, and would
rather pay a small farm than a faceless brand. They use the app to
check on their hive (live video, sensor stats), customize their jars,
and feel connected to a real place.

**Secondary**: anyone curious about bees — gift recipients, casual
hobbyists, family members of the primary user. Lower frequency, but
should land on a screen and immediately understand what's happening.

The job-to-be-done isn't "monitor a sensor stream." It's **stay
emotionally connected to a real hive at a real farm and watch the
honey actually become honey** — with their name on the label.

### Brand Personality

Six concrete words from the brief:

> **warm · earnest · farmhouse · grounded · cozy · a little nerdy**

What that means in practice:

- **Warm + cozy**: cream and honey tones, generous breathing room, no
  cold geometric perfection. The interface should read like a
  small-batch granola label, not a SaaS dashboard.
- **Earnest**: copy is plain and honest. No marketing flourishes, no
  hype words ("revolutionary," "ultimate"). When stats are good, say
  so. When the hive is doing something interesting, explain it like a
  beekeeper at a farmer's market would.
- **Farmhouse + grounded**: tactile, hand-touched, slightly imperfect.
  Imagine a hand-cut sticker on a glass jar, a handwritten ledger of
  yields, a wooden hive frame. The product is a real physical object;
  the app should feel like an extension of that.
- **A little nerdy**: the stats matter. Bees are fascinating. The app
  should treat real-time temperature / humidity / population as
  *interesting* — story-shaped, not just dashboard-shaped. Curiosity,
  not surveillance.

### Aesthetic Direction

Theme strategy: **support both light and dark, but the soul of the
brand is light**. Cream backgrounds with warm honey accents read
"farmhouse." Dark mode is for nighttime use without losing brand —
it's dark-warm (dim honey-tinted near-black), never the
default-AI dark mode (cyan-on-#000).

Already in code:
- Adaptive `BeesColors` palette: honey + cream + charcoal, with light
  and dark variants of every surface
- Serif display via `.system(_, design: .serif)` (SF/New York on iOS) —
  warm without falling into the Fraunces/Cormorant reflex
- Sans body via `.system(_)` (SF Pro) — clean, neutral, lets the serif
  carry the personality

If we ever move off system fonts: target warm humanist serifs that
*aren't* on the reflex list — e.g. Recoleta, Reckless, PP Editorial
New, Tiempos. Avoid Fraunces, Newsreader, Crimson, Cormorant,
Playfair, Lora.

Reference materials we'd want this app to feel adjacent to:

- A Heath Ceramics catalog
- A small-batch honey jar with a letterpressed label
- The Seed phone app's tactile-natural feel
- Apple's Health app for *clarity* (but not its flatness)
- A farmer's market chalkboard

Anti-references (what it must NOT feel like):

- Crypto / Web3 / glassmorphism
- Corporate ag-tech enterprise dashboard
- Generic SaaS with cyan-on-dark gradients
- Cold luxury minimalism (it's farmhouse, not Helsinki design studio)
- "Modern" in the AI-template sense (rounded card grids, gradient
  hero titles, side-stripe accents)

### Design Principles

1. **Warmth over precision.** Hand-touched beats geometric. A slightly
   uneven baseline is more on-brand than a perfect grid.
2. **Provenance is the product.** Surface the farm, the bees, the
   people, the *journey* of honey from hive to jar. Don't hide it
   behind UI.
3. **Stats are stories, not surveillance.** When showing data, frame
   it as something to be curious about — not a number to optimize.
4. **Comfort over density.** Generous breathing room; never cram more
   onto a screen because it fits. The hive doesn't hurry; the app
   shouldn't either.
5. **Quietly confident.** Earnest, never loud. No marketing hype, no
   exclamation marks, no "🎉 Welcome!" Treat the user like an adult
   who chose this on purpose.

### Accessibility

iOS standard expectations apply:
- Dynamic Type support where text content is meaningful (not for
  precise visual elements like the jar studio carousel)
- VoiceOver labels on every interactive surface; the hive reveal
  already has one. Audit the rest.
- 44×44pt minimum touch targets
- Reduced motion respected (already wired in `HiveRevealView` and the
  launch animation should be checked)
- Color-blind-safe state semantics — never rely on color alone to
  signal status (e.g., the health pill should also use icon + label)
